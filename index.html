I'm a Postdoctoral Research Fellow in the EdinburghNLP group (part of the Institute for Language, Cognition and Computation, School of Informatics, University of Edinburgh). I am currently working in collaboration with Prof. Mirella Lapata and Megagon Labs.

My research focuses on deep learning methods for understanding long-form text with applications on summarization, opinion mining and question answering. I am particularly interested in applying novel solutions to language understanding problems where task-specific supervision is scarce.

One of the main objectives of my ongoing research is to develop unsupervised or weakly supervised methodologies for discovering and summarizing popular opinions found in user-generated text.

I succesfully completed my PhD in April 2019, under the supervision of Prof. Mirella Latapa and Prof. Charles Sutton (Thesis: "Weakly Supervised Sentiment Analysis and Opinion Extraction"). Before my PhD, I obtained a MSc in Artificial Intelligence with distinction from the University of Edinburgh. My MSc Thesis was supervised by Prof. Victor Lavrenko. I received my undergraduate degree on Informatics and Telecommunucations from the National and Kapodistrian University of Athens.
